Conversations are the core unit of memory in Memorybase. Every chat you have with ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini is captured as a structured record with full message history and AI-generated metadata.

## AI enrichment

After capture, an LLM automatically extracts **tags** (keywords), **topics** (broader themes), and a **summary** for each conversation. You can supplement these with your own `userTags`, `userTopics`, and `userSummary`.

Processing status is tracked in `metadata.status` — once it reads `completed`, enrichment is done.

## Viewing and browsing

Open the extension popup and go to **Conversations**. Listed in reverse-chronological order, filterable by platform. Click any conversation to see its summary, tags, topics, message count, and linked projects.

Messages are paginated inside the detail view. Each shows role (`user`/`assistant`), content, and timestamp.

## Archiving

Archive a conversation to hide it from the default list without deleting it. All metadata, messages, and project links are preserved. Unarchive anytime from the **Archived** filter.

<Note>
  Archiving does not remove a conversation from projects. Remove it from the project separately if needed.
</Note>

## Linking to projects

From a conversation detail view, click **Add to project** to associate it with one or more projects. You can also add conversations in bulk from the project side — see [Projects](/features/projects).
