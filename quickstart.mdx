---
title: "Quickstart: Install and capture your first memory"
description: "Install the Memorybase Chrome extension, sign in with your email using a one-time code, and capture your first AI conversation in minutes."
---

Memorybase runs as a Chrome extension that you load directly from your local build. Once installed and signed in, it captures AI conversations automatically — no configuration needed to get started.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Download the extension">
    Download the latest Memorybase extension release and unzip it to a folder on your machine.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Load the extension in Chrome">
    Open Chrome and navigate to `chrome://extensions`. Enable **Developer mode** using the toggle in the top-right corner.

    Click **Load unpacked**, then select the unzipped extension folder.

    The Memorybase extension icon appears in your Chrome toolbar.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Sign in with your email">
    Click the Memorybase icon in the Chrome toolbar. The onboarding screen appears with a **Sign In** button.

    Click **Sign In**. A browser tab opens with the Memorybase sign-in page.

    Enter your email address and click **Continue**. Memorybase sends a 6-digit one-time code to that address.

    <Tip>
      Check your spam folder if the code doesn't arrive within a minute or two. The code expires after 10 minutes.
    </Tip>

    Enter the 6-digit code on the sign-in page and click **Sign in**. Once verified, the page shows a confirmation and you can close the tab. The extension popup updates automatically — no page refresh needed.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Capture your first conversation">
    Visit [chatgpt.com](https://chatgpt.com) or [claude.ai](https://claude.ai) and have a conversation as you normally would. Memorybase captures the exchange in the background.

    You'll see the extension icon pulse briefly when a conversation is captured. Click the icon to open the popup and confirm your memory count has increased.

    <Note>
      The first time Memorybase generates embeddings, it loads a small on-device language model (~22 MB). This is a one-time download. Search and injection features become available once the model finishes loading.
    </Note>
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What happens after capture

Once a conversation is captured, Memorybase:

1. **Sanitizes PII on-device** — names, emails, and other identifiable data are scrubbed before anything leaves your browser.
  2. **Generates embeddings locally** — a semantic vector is created for each conversation turn using a model that runs entirely in your browser, enabling fast similarity search with no external API calls.
3. **Syncs to the cloud** — the sanitized conversation is sent to Memorybase's backend, where it's enriched with tags, topics, and a summary.
4. **Becomes searchable** — the conversation appears in your memory library and is available for context injection on any device.

<Warning>
  Memorybase only captures conversations on sites you've signed in to with your Memorybase account. If you sign out of the extension, capture stops until you sign back in.
</Warning>
